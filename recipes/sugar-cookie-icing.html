Sugar Cookie Icing

Description

This sugar cookie icing dries hard and shiny and the colors stay bright. Choose as many different food colorings as you desire. This recipe makes enough icing to decorate a dozen sugar cookies.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of confectioners' sugar: This sugar cookie icing starts with confectioners' sugar (also known as powdered or icing sugar), which creates a smoother texture than granulated sugar.
  • 2 teaspoons of milk: Whole milk thins the icing and ensures a spreadable, easy-to-work-with consistency.
  • 2 teaspoons of corn syrup: Light corn syrup adds shine and helps the icing harden.
  • 1/4 teaspoon of almond extract: A dash of clear almond extract lends subtle nutty flavor. You can substitute vanilla extract if you like (make sure to use clear extract if you're going for a bright white color).
  • Food Coloring (Optional): Depending on how you plan to decorate your cookies, you might want to add a few drops of food coloring or food gel.

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Stir together confectioners' sugar and milk in a small bowl until smooth.
  3. Beat in corn syrup and almond extract until icing is smooth and glossy. If icing is too thick, add more corn syrup.
  4. Divide into separate bowls, and add food colorings to each to desired intensity.
  5. Dip cookies, or paint them with a brush.
